Section 2 · Classification Framework

Three-Category RFI Classification

Every product, workflow, and roadmap is differentiated by category. All 50 UP DCCBs map to one of these three — most fall in PD-RFI. S-20

🌟
Category 1 · DM-RFI
Digitally Mature
~4 UP DCCBs · 55–70 nationally · 15–20% of 351 S-01
All 10 conditions must be met:
Full CBS at 100% branches — real-time, not batch mode
Active UPI endpoint: >1,000 monthly transactions for 12+ months
RuPay card issued to >60% of savings account holders
Aadhaar eKYC operational at ALL branch points
PACS ERP integration live for >50% of PACS
NACH/eMandate for >30% of loan repayment accounts
MIS reporting within 3 working days of period-end
Dedicated IT officer on staff (not vendor-dependent)
Advisory Posture: "Optimisation and Advanced AI Integration" — API enablement, satellite NDVI credit scoring, PACS 100% completion, FPO supply chain finance.
⚡
Category 2 · PD-RFI
Partially Digitalised
~32 UP DCCBs · 195–210 nationally · 55–60% of 351 S-01
Has some, lacks others:
✓ CBS live at HQ and most branches; some still offline
✓ Aadhaar eKYC at HQ minimum only — NOT at PACS level
✓ RuPay card issued to 30–60% of savings holders
✗ No functional UPI endpoint or mobile banking channel
✗ PACS ERP <50% linked; significant data quality issues
✗ NPA tracking: periodic manual Excel reviews only
✗ CRAR/SLR: still fully Excel-based with no automation
✗ No active BC agent micro-ATM deployment
Advisory Posture: "Completion and Consolidation" — PACS data quality, seasonal NACH, BC micro-ATM rollout, semi-automated compliance engine.
🌱
Category 3 · PDC-RFI
Pre-Digital Commencing
~14 UP DCCBs · 85–90 nationally · ~25% of 351 S-01
Limiting conditions present:
CBS at HQ but <60% of branches live and operational
OR CBS in batch mode — no real-time inter-branch capability
No Aadhaar eKYC deployed at any branch point
No UPI, mobile banking, or digital payment channel
RuPay card below 20% of savings account holders
All PACS on paper registers — zero digital linkage
Internet unreliable at 40%+ of branches
CRAR/SLR fully manual — no CBS data extract at all
Advisory Posture: "Foundation First" — CBS stabilisation → staff literacy → Aadhaar eKYC → AePS → basic compliance automation.

Annexure A

Glossary of Key Terms — 28 Definitions

AePS+
Aadhaar-enabled Payment System — NPCI biometric cash transaction. Works without smartphone, PIN, or literacy. Requires only Aadhaar fingerprint at BC agent's micro-ATM device. The most rural-appropriate digital payment for Segment C UP clients. BC commission: ₹1–2 per transaction. S-03
ADEI+
Annual Digital Effectiveness Index — Shared Services/NABARD composite digital maturity score (0–100) published annually per DCCB. PDC-RFI: below 30. PD-RFI: 30–60. DM-RFI: above 60. Category upgrade decision triggered when threshold crossed. S-19
BBPS+
Bharat Bill Payment System — NPCI interoperable utility bill collection. Commission: ₹3–5 per transaction. A PACS serving 3,000 UP households with 2 bills/month earns ₹24,000/month — a self-sustaining incentive for PACS digital adoption requiring no subsidy. S-03
BC+
Business Correspondent — DCCB-appointed doorstep banking agent. Monthly income (active BC): ₹10,950–18,450 (AePS + BBPS + account opening + KCC assistance). Device cost: smartphone + micro-ATM (₹12,000–15,000). NABARD BC training: 3-day certification. S-03
CBS+
Core Banking Solution — centralised software managing all bank operations. All cooperative banks CBS-compliant (RBI mandate, March 2025). Most UP DCCBs use only 40–60% of CBS capability. Shared Services CBS sprint activates unused modules at zero new procurement cost. S-11
CRAR+
Capital to Risk-weighted Assets Ratio = (T1+T2)/RWA × 100. Minimum 9% for cooperative banks (RBI 2024). Currently 40+ person-hours of manual Excel computation per cycle in most UP DCCBs. Compliance engine automates this to 2 hours at DM-RFI level. S-08
DM-RFI+
Digitally Mature RFI — Category 1. ~4 UP DCCBs; 55–70 nationally. All 10 CBS/digital criteria met. Shared Services posture: AI integration, satellite NDVI, FPO supply chain finance, Shared Services SOC. ADEI target: 75+ by Year 3. S-01
ECL+
Expected Credit Loss (IFRS 9) = PD × LGD × EAD. PD: Probability of Default. LGD: Loss Given Default (~40–60% for secured agri loans). EAD: Exposure at Default (outstanding balance). Foundation of the NPA EWS provisioning model used in DM-RFI. S-08
e-NWR+
Electronic Negotiable Warehouse Receipt — WDRA digital receipt enabling post-harvest collateral lending. UP farmer deposits wheat/rice → e-NWR issued → DCCB lends 70–80% of market value → farmer repays after selling at better post-harvest price. Eliminates distress selling at harvest.
EWS+
Early Warning System — automated NPA flagging before the 90-day DPD threshold. Orange-stage accounts recover at 78% vs 42% at Red/NPA stage. Shared Services target: 20–25% NPA formation reduction. DM-RFI uses ML (XGBoost + Random Forest); PD-RFI uses rules-based engine. S-14
FPO+
Farmer Producer Organisation — legally registered farmer collective. Highest-ROI DCCB client segment. FPO management has digital literacy; working capital loans secured by commodity receivables. NABARD RuralTech CoLab (GFF 2025) co-creating FPO digital lending models. S-01
GBPEM+
Generic Bank Product Effectiveness Model — 5-axis scoring framework (PMS · RAS · ICI · FIQ · CVDS). Products <15/25 should not be independently deployed by DCCBs. Products 18–25 are the priority set. Prevents ₹4,000 crore YONO-equivalent mistakes in cooperative banking.
KCC+
Kisan Credit Card — revolving crop credit for Indian farmers; anchor product of every DCCB. Current UP DCCB TAT: 14–42 days. Digital LOS target: 2–5 days. KCC NPA rate: 8–12% currently; AI scoring targets 3–5%. PMFBY auto-enrolment at KCC disbursal is the key insurance linkage. S-15
LOS+
Loan Origination System — digital workflow from application to disbursal. DM-RFI integrates: Aadhaar eKYC → Bhulekh land records → PM Kisan DB → CIBIL → satellite NDVI → weather API → PMFBY auto-enrol. PD-RFI: hybrid. PDC-RFI: paper scan + digital disbursal. S-15
NACH+
National Automated Clearing House — NPCI bulk payment for EMI/mandate debit. Critical UP adaptation: seasonal variable-date mandate for kharif (Oct–Nov) and rabi (Apr–May) harvest cycles. Standard monthly NACH fails for agricultural repayment. Collection cost: ₹2–5 NACH vs ₹50–80 field officer visit. S-03
NABARD+
National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development — apex development bank for rural India. Infused ₹42,000+ crore into cooperative banks FY 2024–25. NABARD TAP covers 96 weak RCBs from April 2025. Operates OSS (Off-Site Surveillance) for cooperative bank monitoring. S-01
NDVI+
Normalised Difference Vegetation Index — ISRO NRSC satellite-derived crop health metric. EWS Orange alert triggered when NDVI drops >15% below seasonal average. Used in DM-RFI AI credit scoring for UP wheat and sugarcane crops. Combined with IMD weather API for comprehensive agri-risk assessment. S-14
NPA+
Non-Performing Asset — loan with principal or interest unpaid for 90+ consecutive days (RBI definition). UP DCCB KCC NPA rate: 8–12%. DM-RFI target: 4% (AI scoring + EWS). PD-RFI target: 6% (rules-based EWS). ECL provisioning: PD × LGD × EAD per IFRS 9. S-08
PACS+
Primary Agricultural Credit Society — village-level cooperative; last-mile credit delivery. 63,000 functional PACS nationally. In UP, PACS data arrives at DCCBs on paper registers. Digitising PACS is the prerequisite for AI in DCCBs. National ERP project linking 63,000 PACS to DCCBs and NABARD. S-07
PD-RFI+
Partially Digitalized RFI — Category 2. ~32 UP DCCBs; 195–210 nationally. CBS live but underutilised (~40–60% of capability). Shared Services posture: Completion and Consolidation. Priority: PACS data quality, seasonal NACH, BC micro-ATM, semi-automated compliance. ADEI target: 60+ by Year 3. S-01
PDC-RFI+
Pre-Digital Commencing RFI — Category 3. ~14 UP DCCBs; 85–90 nationally. CBS partial or batch mode. Shared Services posture: Foundation First. CBS stabilisation → staff literacy → Aadhaar eKYC → AePS → basic compliance. ADEI target: 30+ by Year 3 (triggers PD-RFI upgrade). S-01
PMFBY+
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana — crop insurance. Government premium subsidy: 85%+. Farmer pays only 1.5–2% of sum insured. DM-RFI auto-enrolment at KCC disbursal: 98%+ target. Current UP DCCB rate: <40%. Closing this gap is one of the easiest and highest-impact wins available. S-18
SLR+
Statutory Liquidity Ratio = SLR Securities / NDTL × 100. Minimum 18.00% (RBI, 2024). Compliance engine triggers alert at 18.50% — before breaching the 18% floor. Currently computed manually in Excel in most UP DCCBs. 10 hours manual → real-time automated (DM-RFI) = 99% reduction. S-08
Shared Services+
NABARD Shared Services Platform — NABARD's shared services entity backed by Ministry of Cooperation + NCDC. Targets 130 million cooperative banking customers. ₹1,500 crore annual savings from Year 5. Serves all 351 DCCBs. Delivers 88% cost reduction through shared infrastructure vs standalone procurement. S-19
UPI+
Unified Payments Interface — NPCI real-time payment. 10.17 billion+ monthly transactions (peak 2024). UP rural client pathway: USSD *99# (feature phone, zero data) → AePS (biometric only) → UPI QR at PACS → UPI 123PAY (feature-phone UPI) → full mobile UPI app. S-03
USSD+
Unstructured Supplementary Service Data — *99# banking on any GSM phone without internet. Zero data cost. Balance inquiry, mini-statement, fund transfer. First digital product for all PDC-RFI DCCBs. Deployable in 30–45 days at ₹50K–1 lakh setup cost. Serves 100% of UP's feature-phone users. S-03
V-CIP+
Video Customer Identification Process — RBI-approved remote KYC via video call (2020 framework). Enables rural account opening without branch visit. Critical for UP migrant workers: city worker opens home-DCCB account remotely for family remittance receipt. Traditional KYC: 2–5 days → V-CIP: 5–10 minutes. DM-RFI initially. S-02
VAPT+
Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing — RBI-mandated annual security testing for all banks. Individual DCCB cost: ₹1–3 lakhs/year. Shared Services empanels approved agencies for cooperative banks. Cybersecurity framework for cooperative banks issued by RBI in 2023–24 circular. S-17
WDRA+
Warehousing Development and Regulatory Authority — operates the e-NWR (electronic Negotiable Warehouse Receipt) registry. HDFC, ICICI, Axis Bank offer warehouse receipt finance against e-NWRs commercially. For UP grain-belt DCCBs (wheat, rice, pulses): enables post-harvest lending without distress selling at harvest.
